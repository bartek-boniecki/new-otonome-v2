07specific objections

Answered straight.

Not marketing FAQ. Questions every prospect actually asks on call one.

We tried this internally for two weeks and gave up. What's different?
Internal attempts fail at the same step: attribution turns out to be a two-week side project that competes with shipping the product. The engineer with the context gets pulled back to feature work in week one. We do nothing else. We have a checklist, working patterns for every common gateway, and no incentive to scope-creep into a year-long thing.
How do you prove the savings aren't just attribution shifting?
Every change ships with a before/after measurement plan defined in week 1 and signed off by your team. Savings compare like-for-like traffic on the same workload over the same window, with quality verified via evals you approve. We hand over the dashboards — you can re-run the math anytime. If you can't reproduce a number, we don't bill against it.
What happens when you leave?
Everything was built in your repo, with your tools, against your accounts, from day one. There is no basis-hosted anything. The handoff includes runbooks, on-call docs, and the eval suite in CI. The 30-day check-in answers questions, not extends the engagement. Most clients don't need us again. That's the goal.
Our spend is "only" $10–20k/month. Is this worth it?
Probably not yet — and we'll tell you on the free audit call. Below ~$15k/mo, your time is better spent shipping the next feature than optimizing the current one. The math works once a 40% cut is meaningfully larger than the cost of the work. We tell people to come back later more often than you'd expect.

The audit is free. The diagnosis is written. The decision is yours.
